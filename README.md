Project Introduction 

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges faced by banks and financial institutions. Losing existing customers not only reduces revenue but also increases the cost of acquiring new customers. This project focuses on developing a predictive analytics system that identifies customers who are likely to leave the bank and assigns a churn risk score to each customer using machine learning techniques.

The objective of the project is to analyze customer behavior, transaction history, demographic information, and banking activity to predict churn probability in advance. By detecting high-risk customers early, banks can implement targeted retention strategies, personalized offers, and customer engagement programs to improve customer loyalty and reduce attrition.

The system uses historical banking data containing features such as account balance, credit score, transaction frequency, loan status, customer tenure, product usage, complaints, and digital banking activity. Data preprocessing techniques including handling missing values, feature encoding, normalization, and outlier treatment are applied to prepare the dataset for modeling.

Advantages of Predictive Modeling and Risk Scoring for Bank Customer Churn
Early Identification of Churning Customers

The project helps banks identify customers who are likely to leave before they actually churn, allowing proactive action.
Improved Customer Retention
By predicting customer behavior, banks can implement personalized retention strategies such as special offers, loyalty rewards, or better services.
Increased Revenue
Retaining existing customers is more cost-effective than acquiring new ones, which helps improve overall profitability.
Better Decision Making
The predictive model provides data-driven insights that support management in making strategic business decisions.
Risk-Based Customer Segmentation
Customers can be classified into Low, Medium, and High churn-risk groups, helping banks prioritize retention efforts efficiently.
Enhanced Customer Experience
Understanding customer needs and dissatisfaction factors allows banks to improve services and customer satisfaction.
Reduction in Marketing Costs
Banks can focus retention campaigns only on high-risk customers instead of targeting all customers, reducing unnecessary expenses.
Identification of Key Churn Factors
The system analyzes patterns and identifies major reasons for customer attrition such as poor service, low account activity, or high charges.
Automation of Churn Analysis
Machine learning automates the prediction process, reducing manual effort and improving operational efficiency.
Scalable and Adaptable Solution
The model can handle large banking datasets and can be updated with new customer data for continuous improvement.
Competitive Advantage
Banks using predictive analytics can improve customer loyalty and stay ahead of competitors in the financial sector.
